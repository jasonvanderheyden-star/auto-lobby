# Naming decision — parked

**Resume after Phase 3 ships** (Monthly Certification UI live with ≥1 paying customer or design partner under contract).

## Scope expanded — 2026-04-30

The original framing of this doc was "rename Auto Lobby." That framing is now too narrow. After the agency-motion conversation and the platform-vision conversation (see `docs/Agency-Motion-Parked.md` and `docs/Platform-Vision-Parked.md`), the actual ambition is:

> A multi-product **regulatory operating system for Canadian regulated industries.** Auto Lobby is the first product, not the company.

So the naming exercise to revisit is now:

1. **Parent brand / company name.** The umbrella that houses Auto Lobby + future products (Policy Track, Reg Map, Consultation Tracker, Procurement Intelligence, etc.). This is the bigger commitment — needs to scale across compliance + intelligence + permitting + filings without sounding gimmicky.
2. **Product-naming convention.** Distinct names per product (parent = "X", products = "Lobby" / "Track" / "Map" / etc.) or branded suffix ("Auto Lobby" / "Auto Permit" / "Auto Track")? "Auto X" doesn't scale gracefully past compliance.
3. **Auto Lobby's own future.** Stays as product name? Renames to fit the convention? TBD as part of this exercise.

The five candidates below were evaluated for product-naming voice, not parent-brand-of-platform voice. They may or may not still apply when the exercise resumes. Specifically:

- **Docket** — feels more product-name-ish than parent-brand-ish; could work as a single-product brand
- **Probity** — probably stronger as a parent brand (virtue/trust signal works at company level)
- **Plicit** — invented-word, equally fits product or parent
- **Lode** — could work either level; mining/lodestar metaphor is broad
- **Chancel** — religious connotation likely disqualifying for a multi-product platform

When the exercise resumes, the candidate slate will likely need to be re-opened with the parent-brand criteria layered in.

## Where we landed

After two rounds of WebSearch domain/brand conflict checks across 27 candidates, five names survived with no obvious SaaS collision:

| Name | Vibe | Strength |
|---|---|---|
| **Docket** | Legal/gov — "on the docket" | Best gov-DNA; short; verbs well |
| **Probity** | Virtue — integrity, honesty | Strong trust signal; slightly formal/long |
| **Plicit** | Coined from implicit/explicit | Invented-word freedom; empty vessel for brand |
| **Lode** | Mining — "mother lode," lodestar | Evocative; not gov-specific |
| **Chancel** | Architectural — front of church | Uncommon but religious connotation may confuse |

Claude's ranking: **Docket** > **Plicit** > **Probity** > **Lode** > **Chancel**.

## What we killed (and why)

Across two rounds, these all died due to direct SaaS conflicts — notably a cluster inside our own category:

- **Quorum** — direct competitor. [quorum.us](https://www.quorum.us/) is a public-affairs / lobbying / PAC compliance SaaS. Add to competitive context.
- **Beacon** — PwC Beacon (compliance), Beacon Healthcare (compliance), ESA Beacon (compliance). The word is saturated in compliance SaaS.
- **Accord** — multiple compliance SaaSes called Accord including ACA compliance and contract management.
- **Warden** — Compliance Warden, Warden AI (fair/compliant AI).
- **Obligo** — fintech + separate contract-management tool.
- Others killed: Herald, Vellum, Sigil, Almanac, Attest, Avow, Rubric, Scrivener, Emblem, Solon, Nomos, Axia, Portico, Cairn, Lumen, Pando, Tern, Parley, Acta, Edict, Folio, Curia, Dispatch, Writ (Contentful acquisition), Decree (Decree.dev AI task mgmt).

Early-round (French/bilingual) dropped per Jason's "must work beyond Canada": Mandat, Marque, Rapport.

## Next step when resuming (revised for expanded scope)

This is now a focused 2–3 day workstream, not a 30-minute brainstorm. Sequence:

1. **Re-open the candidate slate** with parent-brand criteria layered in:
   - Works as platform / company brand (not just one product)
   - Accommodates future products (compliance, monitoring, permitting, filings)
   - Doesn't lock us into a single category
   - International-friendly (Canada-first, but not Canada-only)
   - Available .com (the prize) + .ca (defensive)
   - Trademark clear in CIPO + USPTO

2. **Re-evaluate the surviving five** through the parent-brand lens. Likely outcome: 1–2 graduate, 3–4 get demoted to "could be a product name within the parent."

3. **Generate ~30–50 fresh candidates** with the broader brief (parent brand for a regulatory platform). Domain availability + trademark sweep on each.

4. **Domain hunt.** Paste finalists into Namecheap / GoDaddy / domain brokers. Expect .com's on attractive parent-brand names to be premium ($5K–$50K). Budget accordingly.

5. **Decide product-naming convention.** Branded suffix ("Auto Lobby" + "Auto Permit") vs. distinct family ("Lobby" + "Track" + "Map"). This decision constrains parent-brand requirements.

6. **Lock parent brand**, then decide Auto Lobby's fate (keep, rename to fit convention, or rename + retire).

7. **Brand system.** Logo, typography (Geist or Inter), color system, voice. Likely a designer engagement at this stage.
