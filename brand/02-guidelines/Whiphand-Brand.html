01 · The name

Whiphand

From the idiom “to have the whip hand,” meaning to hold control and the decisive advantage.

It carries a second, quieter meaning: in Parliament, the whip is the officer who keeps the agenda and the votes in order. Both senses point the same way. Whiphand puts you in command of a system that normally controls you. The name is confident, not aggressive: it’s about control, not force.

/ˈwɪp ˌhand/ · noun · “positive control; advantage,” per Merriam-Webster

02 · Positioning & voice

What we are

Whiphand automates the work between an organization and its government. The first offering, Auto Lobby, handles lobbying compliance: it watches your calendar, finds meetings with officials, drafts the monthly report, and leaves one action a month, review and approve. Intelligence, grants, and permits follow on the same platform.

How we sound

Plain and precise, with a calm sense of authority. We keep the user in control.

We never use jargon or acronyms.

“your meetings”  not  “calendar events”
“officials”  not  “DPOHs”
“monthly report”  not  “MCR”
“the lobbying registry”  not  “OCL / LRS”

PRIMARY TAGLINE

The whip hand over government.

Alternates: “Government affairs, under control.” · “The operating system for government & regulatory affairs.”

PRIMARY HORIZONTAL LOCKUP

Do

Keep clear space around the mark equal to the height of its head. Use the wordmark lowercase. Slate-on-mist, or white-on-ink.

Don’t

Recolor the mace outside the palette, stretch it, add effects, capitalize the wordmark, or place it on busy backgrounds.
